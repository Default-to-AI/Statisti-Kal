# Statisti-Kal — Trademark Acceptable Use Policy

This document explains what you may and may not do with the **"Statisti-Kal" name, logo, and brand identifiers**. It is intended to make forking easy while keeping the brand honest.

The **source code license** is governed separately by `LICENSE.md`. This document governs trademarks and other brand identifiers only.

This is a public, plain-language policy. It is **not** a substitute for professional legal advice. For commercial or institutional questions contact `license@statisti-kal.com`.

---

## 1. Reserved brand identifiers

The Licensor (Statisti-Kal Project) reserves the following identifiers:

- **The word mark "Statisti-Kal"** — in English and in its Hebrew rendering. Avoid confusingly similar variants that mimic the distinctive hyphenated form.
- **The Statisti-Kal logo** — including the bell-curve monogram and any wordmark lockup used in the project.
- **The product's distinctive visual presentation** — when used as a source-identifying arrangement in the official deployment. Raw hex tokens (for example `#D4A843` and `#4ECDC4`) are not claimed in isolation; what is reserved is the use of these elements in the specific ornamental arrangement that identifies the official product to end users.

When these elements appear in a hosted tool as identification, they signal to the user that the tool is the official Statisti-Kal product. Only the Licensor and its explicit licensees may use them for product identification.

## 2. What is NOT claimed

The Licensor respects that good ideas should be reusable. The following are NOT trademarked and you are free to adopt them in any fork:

- **General UI conventions** — dark-mode aesthetics, generous whitespace, KaTeX-style math rendering, RTL Hebrew interfaces, conventions for input groups, table layouts, accordion patterns, and similar functional patterns.
- **Color values used in isolation** — raw hex tokens for brass, teal, indigo, and the dark-mode palette. They may be reused for any purpose.
- **Generic design patterns** — calculators that compute hypothesis tests, normal distributions, point estimates, or linear regressions. Functional ideas are not monopolized.
- **Mathematical concepts, definitions, and formulas** — these belong to mathematical tradition, not to any one product.

When in doubt: copy the ideas and the code, but rename the product and replace the logo. That is always permitted.

## 3. Forks and unmodified-code deployments

If you redistribute or self-host the Licensed Work under `LICENSE.md`, you MUST:

1. **Use your own name and brand.** Do not call your fork "Statisti-Kal" or any confusingly similar name (for example "StatistiKall", "Statistikal", "Statisti-Cal", or a Hebrew rendering that mimics the distinctive form). The aim is for downstream users to be able to tell which product they are using.
2. **Use your own logo.** Replace the bell-curve monogram and wordmark with a distinct logo of your own. You may keep the visual style conventions described in §2; you may not keep the identifying marks.
3. **Preserve the original copyright and license notices** in any distributed binary or snapshot, AND add a prominent notice clarifying that your fork is a community edition, not the official product.
4. **Avoid suggesting endorsement.** Do not use phrases like "the Statisti-Kal project supports this fork" or any wording that suggests the Statisti-Kal Project endorses, sponsors, or has reviewed your fork, unless you have written permission from the Licensor.

## 4. Permitted uses (no separate trademark license required)

The following do NOT require a separate trademark license. The Licensor will not assert rights against:

- **Educational previews and reviews.** Linking to, embedding screenshots of, or describing Statisti-Kal in a blog post, university course, tutorial, lecture, scientific paper, news article, conference talk, or academic thesis is welcome.
- **Criticism, parody, and commentary.** Use of the name to identify what your article or video is *about* (for example "A critique of Statisti-Kal's normal-distribution calculator") under standard fair-use principles.
- **Aggregation by search engines and directories.** Search-result listings, code-search providers, and public indexes may surface the project under its brand without licenses.
- **Cohort and study-group sharing.** Sharing the official hosted URL (not the source code itself) inside a student WhatsApp / Telegram / Facebook study group is unrestricted.
- **Editorial screenshots in textbooks.** Stating "this textbook uses Statisti-Kal screenshots" with proper attribution. Books are encouraged to request formal press-kit materials via `press@statisti-kal.com` for high-resolution assets.
- **Open-source code contributions.** Contributors to the official repository may display the project name, logo, and screenshots in contributions, commits, and PR descriptions, subject to standard conduct rules.

## 5. Reserved uses (permission required)

All other brand uses are RESERVED to the Licensor and require permission. This typically includes:

- **Merchandise** (t-shirts, mugs, stickers, etc.) using the logo or wordmark.
- **Domain names** that incorporate "Statisti-Kal" outside clearly non-commercial review contexts.
- **Apps submitted to app stores** under any name containing the mark.
- **Paid marketing or sponsored placements** that use the Statisti-Kal mark to suggest product endorsement.
- **Bundling the Statisti-Kal brand with paid offerings** (for example paid bootcamps, paid tutoring services, paid SAT/GRE-style Hebrew statistics courses) in a way that suggests Statisti-Kal endorsement.
- **Forks or hosted self-hosting under the Statisti-Kal name** (per §3).

To request a trademark license for any of these use cases, contact `license@statisti-kal.com`.

## 6. Investigation / takedown

The Licensor's strong preference is education and conversation ahead of formal action. If you believe a fork is in violation of this policy, open a public issue in the project's repository and tag the maintainers. Other forks can usually be resolved by polite renaming. Formal legal action is a last resort, used only when a violation is consumer-harmful, commercially deceptive in Israel or another market, or conducted in bad faith.
